## Regulatory References

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

**Full Name**: Regulation (EU) 2016/679

**Key Articles**:
- **Article 5**: Principles (lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, accountability)
- **Article 6**: Legal basis for processing
- **Article 7**: Conditions for consent
- **Article 12-14**: Transparency and information to data subject
- **Article 15**: Right of access
- **Article 16**: Right to rectification
- **Article 17**: Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- **Article 18**: Right to restrict processing
- **Article 20**: Right to data portability
- **Article 21**: Right to object
- **Article 22**: Rights related to automated decision-making
- **Article 24-32**: Responsibility of controller and security
- **Article 33**: Notification of a personal data breach
- **Article 34**: Communication of a personal data breach
- **Article 35-36**: Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
- **Article 37**: Data Protection Officer
- **Article 46**: Transfers subject to appropriate safeguards

**Official Text**: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/en/eli/reg/2016/679

**EDPB Guidance**: https://edpb.eu/

---

### UK Data Protection Act 2018

**Purpose**: UK implementation of GDPR

**Key Sections**:
- Part 2: General processing
- Part 3: Law enforcement processing
- Part 4: Special purposes (journalism, research)

**Official Text**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12

**ICO Guidance**: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/data-protection-an-overview/

---

### Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003 (PECR)

**Purpose**: UK implementation of ePrivacy Directive

**Key Rules**:
- Consent before marketing calls, texts, emails
- Cookies and similar technologies require consent
- Exceptions for essential cookies

**Official Text**: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2426

**ICO Guidance**: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/marketing/

---

### California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

**Official Name**: California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) - Updated 2020

**Key Rights**:
- Right to Know (what data is collected)
- Right to Delete (erasure)
- Right to Opt-Out (of sale/sharing)
- Right to Correct (fix inaccurate data)
- Right to Non-Discrimination

**Applies To**: For-profit businesses that collect California residents' personal information

**Official Text**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=201720180AB375

**AG Guidance**: https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa

---

### ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC)

**Purpose**: Harmonizes privacy rules for electronic communications

**Key Areas**:
- Cookies and tracking technologies
- Spam and direct marketing
- Confidentiality of communications

**Official Text**: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=CELEX:32002L0058

## Tools & Templates

### Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

**ICO DPIA Template**: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/data-protection-impact-assessments/

**When Required**:
- Large-scale processing
- Systematic monitoring
- Automated decision-making
- Special category data
- High-risk processing

---

### Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)

**EDPB Guidelines**: https://edpb.eu/our-work-tools/documents/categories/guidelines/

**Key Questions**:
1. What is the legitimate interest?
2. Is processing necessary?
3. What is the reasonable expectation?
4. Balance: Does interest override user rights?

---

### Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**:
- EU Commission Decision: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32010D0087

**Processor Obligations**:
- Process only as instructed
- Implement security measures
- Assist with data subject rights
- Notify of breaches
- Allow audits

**Supplementary Measures Required** (post-Schrems II):
- Encryption
- Key management
- Access controls
- Monitoring

### Data Transfer Impact Assessment

**Process**:
1. Identify transfer mechanism (SCC, adequacy decision, derogation)
2. Assess risks to individuals
3. Implement supplementary safeguards
4. Document assessment
